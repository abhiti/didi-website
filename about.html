Kanika Asavari Vaish is a writer, director, actor, and stand-up comedian. She was born in New York, and spent her middle and high school years in Mumbai, India.

She studied English and Political Science at Wellesley College, where she had the pleasure of acting in several Shakespeare productions and directing Rajiv Joseph's Bengal Tiger at the Baghdad Zoo and Guards at the Taj. Her senior thesis, a collection of short stories entitled Jet Lag, advised by poet Octavio Gonzalez, won the Charlotte Paul Reese '38 Memorial Prize for Creativity in a Collection of Short Fiction.

After Wellesley, she moved to Washington, D.C., where she spent two wildly busy years working at an international arbitration law firm, an experience which inspired her comedic play Charles B. Meyer v. The Republic of Malaysia.

She is currently a first-year playwriting/TV writing student at Columbia School of the Arts, under the tutelage of David Henry Hwang, Lynn Nottage, Rogelio Martinez, and Matthew Fennell.

She is also the co-founder of Fresh Lime Soda Productions, a contemporary South Asian theater ensemble based in NYC. Check us out here.

She writes about impressionable youth, ghosts, nostalgia, modern love, intergenerational dynamics, and her Indian heritage, and she is passionate about creating work for queer, trans, and differently-abled artists and audiences of color.

If you'd like to get to know her work better or collaborate on a project, please feel free to reach out.
