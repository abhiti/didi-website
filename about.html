Kanika Asavari Vaish is a writer, director, actor, and producer. She was born in New York, and spent her middle and high school years in Mumbai, India.

She attended Wellesley College, where she studied English & Political Science, and acted in and directed several on campus productions. Her senior thesis, a collection of short stories entitled Jet Lag, advised by poet Octavio Gonzalez, won the Charlotte Paul Reese '38 Memorial Prize for Creativity in a Collection of Short Fiction.

She is currently an MFA student at Columbia University School of the Arts, studying playwriting under the tutelage of David Henry Hwang, Lynn Nottage, Lisa Kron, Chuck Mee, Deborah Brevoort, Michael Korie, Rogelio Martinez, and Morgan Jenness.

She is also the co-founder and artistic director of Fresh Lime Soda Productions, a contemporary theater ensemble aimed at amplifying new works and fostering community among South Asian artists and performers.

She writes about impressionable youth, nostalgia, mythology, ghosts, modern love, intergenerational dynamics, transformational settings, and her South Asian heritage, and believes in using comedy as a tool for collective healing.

If you'd like to get to know her work better, or collaborate on a project, please feel free to reach out!
